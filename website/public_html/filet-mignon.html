Perfect Filet Mignon

MasterClass Recipe →

Main Course
Fine Dining
Prep Time 15 minutes
Cook Time 15-20 minutes
Total Time 30-35 minutes
Servings 2

Ingredients

  • 2 filet mignon steaks (6-8 oz each, 1½ to 2 inches thick)
  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 3 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • Kosher salt, to taste
  • Fresh cracked black pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons high-heat cooking oil (vegetable or avocado)
  • Optional: Fleur de sel for finishing

Instructions

1Prepare the Steaks

Remove the filet mignon steaks from the refrigerator 30-40 minutes before cooking. Pat them completely dry with paper towels. This step is crucial for achieving a good crust. Season generously with kosher salt and fresh cracked black pepper on both sides.

2Heat the Pan

Heat a heavy-bottomed skillet (cast iron or stainless steel) over high heat until it's smoking hot. This creates the perfect sear. Add the high-heat cooking oil and let it heat until it shimmers, about 1-2 minutes.

3Sear the Steaks

Place the steaks in the hot skillet and sear for 3-4 minutes on the first side without moving them. This creates a golden crust. Flip once and sear the other side for another 3-4 minutes for medium-rare (130-135°F internal temperature).

4Add Aromatics and Finish

Reduce heat to medium. Add the butter, smashed garlic, rosemary, and thyme to the pan. Tilt the pan and baste the steaks with the foaming butter for 2-3 minutes. This adds incredible flavor and helps cook the steaks to your desired doneness.

5Rest and Serve

Remove steaks from the pan and transfer to a warm plate. Rest for 5-10 minutes before serving (this allows the juices to redistribute). Spoon some of the herb butter over the top. Season with fleur de sel if desired. Serve immediately.

📝 Chef's Tips for Perfect Filet Mignon

  • Temperature is Key: Use a meat thermometer for accuracy. Filet mignon is best served medium-rare (130-135°F).
  • Room Temperature: Always bring steaks to room temperature before cooking. This ensures even cooking throughout.
  • Dry Surface: Moisture prevents browning. Pat steaks dry with paper towels before seasoning.
  • Don't Move the Steaks: Resist the urge to move them around. Let them sit undisturbed for a proper crust.
  • Quality Matters: Filet mignon is an elegant cut; invest in high-quality beef from a trusted butcher.
  • Herb Butter Basting: This is what elevates the dish. Don't skip this step.
  • Resting Period: Never skip the rest. It makes a huge difference in tenderness and juiciness.
  • Thickness Matters: 1½ to 2 inches is ideal for achieving a perfect crust and tender interior.
  • High Heat is Essential: This is not the time for gentle cooking. You need a screaming hot pan.
