Dried Wild Mushroom Risotto

Forager Chef Recipe →

Main Course
Italian
Prep Time 20 minutes
Cook Time 30 minutes
Total Time 50 minutes
Servings 4

Ingredients

  • 40 grams dehydrated wild mushrooms (roughly 2 cups of dried mushroom slices)
  • ¾ teaspoon kosher salt
  • 5 cups stock (highest quality possible, preferably homemade)
  • ¼ cup shallot (diced small)
  • 1.5 cups risotto rice (like carnaroli, arborio, baldo, etc)
  • ½ cup dry white wine
  • Splash of cooking oil
  • 5 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • ¾ cup grated Parmesan (plus more for serving)
  • Fresh chopped Italian parsley (to garnish, optional)

Instructions

1Rehydrate the Mushrooms

Add the mushrooms to the stock and rehydrate for 30 minutes, then agitate to remove grit and swish them a few times. Remove the mushrooms, strain the liquid and reserve both separately, keeping a handful of mushrooms for a finishing garnish if desired.

2Prepare the Rice

Coarsely chop the mushrooms, aiming for sizes that will fit on a spoon. In a wide pan (about 10-12 inches diameter), sweat the shallot on medium-high heat in the oil until translucent, then add the rice and stir to coat with oil. Cook for a few minutes more, but don't allow the bottom of the pan to color.

3Cook the Risotto

Add the wine, mushrooms and salt, and cook until the pan is nearly dry. Then begin ladling in the stock gradually in ½ to ¾ cup increments, waiting until the stock is absorbed before adding the next ladleful.

4Finish and Serve

When the rice is just barely cooked through, add 4 tablespoons of the butter, the Parmesan and parsley. Stir vigorously until incorporated. Taste and adjust the seasoning for salt if needed, and adjust the consistency with a little extra stock if necessary. The risotto should be thick and creamy, but also flowing and loose. Portion into soup bowls, jiggle the sides to flatten, and serve with additional grated Parmesan at the table.

📝 Chef's Tips

  • Quality Stock is Essential: Use the highest quality stock possible, preferably homemade. This is the foundation of an excellent risotto.
  • Dried Mushrooms: The rehydration water contains flavor - that's why we use it as part of the cooking liquid.
  • Constant Attention: Risotto requires patience and attention. Don't rush the process of adding stock gradually.
  • Rice Type Matters: Use risotto-specific rice like carnaroli, arborio, or baldo for the proper creamy texture.
  • Final Creaminess: The risotto should be served immediately and should flow slightly on the plate - not stiff.
  • Mushroom Flavor: Save some mushroom pieces as garnish for visual appeal and concentrated flavor.
  • Pan Size: A wide, shallow pan helps with even cooking and allows for better stirring control.
