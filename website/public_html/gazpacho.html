Easy Gazpacho Recipe

Love and Lemons Recipe →

Soups
Appetizer
Prep Time 10 minutes
Chilling Time 2 hours
Total Time 2 hours 10 minutes
Servings 4 to 6

Ingredients

  • 1 English cucumber (or regular cucumber, peeled)
  • 2½ pounds fresh tomatoes (or 2 cans crushed tomatoes)
  • 1 red bell pepper (or 2 fresno chiles for heat)
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • ¼ red onion (rinsed to reduce sharpness)
  • ½ cup fresh cilantro (optional but recommended)
  • ½ cup extra-virgin olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons red wine vinegar or sherry vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For Serving (Optional Garnishes)

  • Diced cucumber
  • Cherry tomatoes, halved
  • Fresh herbs (cilantro, basil)
  • Extra olive oil
  • Croutons or roasted chickpeas
  • Hard boiled eggs, chopped
  • Avocado slices

Instructions

1Prepare the Vegetables

Dice some of the cucumber and set aside for garnish. Peel the remaining cucumber. Roughly chop the tomatoes, bell pepper, garlic, and red onion.

2Blend the Soup

Add the peeled cucumber, tomatoes, bell pepper, garlic, red onion, cilantro, olive oil, vinegar, salt, and pepper to a blender. Blend until smooth, working in batches if necessary (this recipe makes a lot of soup). You may need to blend in 2-3 batches depending on your blender size.

3Chill the Soup

Transfer the blended gazpacho to a large bowl or pitcher. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours (or up to 3 days). The longer it chills, the more the flavors meld and develop, making it more complex and refreshing. The soup becomes even better on days 2 and 3!

4Adjust Seasoning

Before serving, taste and adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, and vinegar to your preference. The soup should be balanced between tangy, rich, and refreshing.

5Serve

Serve cold in glasses or bowls. Garnish with diced cucumber, cherry tomatoes, fresh herbs, and a drizzle of olive oil. You can also add croutons, roasted chickpeas, hard boiled eggs, or avocado for a more substantial meal.

📝 Chef's Tips

  • Use Quality Tomatoes: The quality of your tomatoes will really affect the final dish. Fresh, ripe tomatoes in summer are best. If using canned, look for high-quality crushed tomatoes.
  • Fresno Chiles vs Bell Pepper: This recipe traditionally uses red bell pepper, but fresno chiles add a wonderful heat. Choose based on your preference.
  • Batch Blending: This recipe makes a lot of soup! Don't hesitate to blend in multiple batches to ensure smooth, even texture.
  • Rinsing the Onion: Rinsing raw red onion under cold water before blending tames its sharp, pungent flavor.
  • Chilling is Essential: The 2-hour chill time is not optional! It allows all the flavors to meld and develop properly. Rushing this step will result in a less flavorful soup.
  • Make Ahead: Gazpacho actually tastes better the longer it sits. Make it the day before and it will be even more delicious!
  • Vegan & Gluten-Free: This recipe is naturally vegan and gluten-free, making it perfect for various dietary preferences.
