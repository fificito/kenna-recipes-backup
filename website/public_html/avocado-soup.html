Avocado Soup

Giangi's Kitchen Recipe →

Soup
Creamy
Prep Time 15 minutes
Cook Time 20 minutes
Total Time 35 minutes
Servings 4

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or butter
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 3 ripe avocados, pitted and peeled
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream or sour cream
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and white pepper to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish
  • Crispy tortilla strips for garnish (optional)
  • Red onion, thinly sliced for garnish (optional)

Instructions

1Sauté Aromatics

Heat olive oil or butter in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook for 3-4 minutes until softened. Add minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant. Don't let the garlic brown—keep the heat moderate.

2Add Broth

Pour in the vegetable or chicken broth. Bring to a gentle simmer and cook for 5 minutes to allow flavors to develop. The broth should be steaming but not boiling vigorously.

3Add Avocados

Remove the pot from heat and let it cool slightly. Cut the avocados into chunks and add them to the pot along with the lime juice. The slight cooling prevents the avocados from becoming bitter from high heat, which can happen if cooked too long at high temperatures.

4Blend Until Smooth

Using an immersion blender, blend the soup until completely smooth and creamy. Work in batches with a regular blender if needed, being careful with the hot liquid. The texture should be silky and velvety. Blend for a minute or two to achieve the creamiest result.

5Add Cream and Season

Stir in the heavy cream or sour cream. Add salt, white pepper, and cayenne pepper if using. Taste and adjust seasonings to your preference. The soup should be balanced between creamy, fresh, and slightly spicy.

6Serve

Ladle the warm soup into bowls. Garnish with fresh cilantro, crispy tortilla strips, and thinly sliced red onion if desired. The contrast of toppings adds texture and visual appeal. Serve immediately while warm, though this soup can also be chilled and served cold on hot days.

📝 Chef's Tips for Perfect Avocado Soup

  • Avocado Ripeness: Use ripe but not overripe avocados. They should yield slightly to pressure. Overripe avocados can taste mushy and slightly bitter. If avocados are hard, let them ripen at room temperature for a day or two.
  • Lime Juice is Key: The lime juice not only adds flavor but also prevents the avocado from oxidizing and turning brown. This is essential for presentation and taste.
  • Don't Overheat: Keep avocados away from high heat. Add them after removing the pot from heat or at the very end. High heat can make them taste bitter and ruin the delicate flavor.
  • Smooth Texture: Blend thoroughly until completely smooth. Any lumps will detract from this elegant soup. Use an immersion blender for best results and control.
  • Cream Options: Heavy cream makes it richer; sour cream adds tang. You can also use Greek yogurt for a lighter version with good flavor.
  • Temperature Flexibility: This soup is delicious warm or chilled. For a cold version, prepare as directed and refrigerate before serving. It keeps well for 2-3 days in the fridge.
  • Garnish Matters: Fresh cilantro, crispy tortilla strips, a drizzle of lime crema, or sliced red onion all enhance the presentation and flavor. Don't skip the garnish!
  • Make to Order: While this soup can be made ahead, avocado can oxidize and brown over time. For best color and flavor, blend the avocado no more than 2-3 hours before serving.
  • Broth Quality: Use good quality broth—homemade or premium brands taste significantly better and make a difference in the final result.
  • Spice Level: The cayenne is optional but adds a nice complexity. Adjust to taste—this soup is creamy and mild, so a little heat works well.
