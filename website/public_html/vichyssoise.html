Classic Vichyssoise

All Recipes Recipe →

Soup
French
Prep Time 20 minutes
Cook Time 45 minutes
Chill Time 2 hours
Servings 6

Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 4 leeks, white part only, cleaned and sliced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 4 potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon white pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 cup heavy cream or crème fraîche
  • 2 tablespoons fresh chives, finely chopped
  • Additional salt and white pepper to taste

Instructions

1Prepare the Vegetables

Clean the leeks thoroughly by slicing them in half lengthwise and rinsing between layers to remove any sand. Slice into half-moons. Peel the potatoes and cut into small dice (about 1/2 inch cubes). The vegetables should be roughly the same size for even cooking.

2Sauté the Leeks and Onion

Melt the butter in a large pot over medium heat. Add the sliced leeks and onion. Cook gently for about 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened and translucent but not browned. This gentle cooking brings out the leeks' natural sweetness without adding color.

3Add Potatoes and Broth

Add the diced potatoes, broth, salt, white pepper, cayenne, and bay leaf. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer gently for 25-30 minutes until the potatoes are very tender and beginning to break down. The soup should simmer gently, not vigorously.

4Blend Until Smooth

Remove the bay leaf. Using an immersion blender, blend the soup until completely smooth and silky. If you don't have an immersion blender, carefully transfer the soup in batches to a regular blender, blend until smooth, and return to the pot. The texture should be velvety without any lumps.

5Add Cream and Season

Stir in the heavy cream. Taste and adjust seasonings with additional salt and white pepper as needed. The soup should taste balanced and slightly delicate—not too salty or peppery. Cook gently for another 2-3 minutes without boiling to combine flavors.

6Chill Thoroughly

Transfer the soup to a bowl and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, preferably 4 hours or overnight. The soup should be served very cold. You can prepare this ahead and keep it refrigerated for up to 2 days. Before serving, taste again and adjust seasonings if needed—cold soup often needs slightly more seasoning than hot soup.

7Serve

Ladle the chilled soup into chilled bowls. Garnish generously with fresh chives. A small dollop of crème fraîche or sour cream on top is traditional and adds elegance. Serve immediately while very cold. The presentation should be pristine and refined—this is a classic French soup meant to impress.

📝 Chef's Tips for Perfect Vichyssoise

  • Leek Selection: Use only the white and light green parts of the leeks. The dark green parts are too tough and can make the soup bitter. Clean them very carefully as sand can hide between layers.
  • Smooth Texture: Blend until completely silky smooth. A slightly grainy texture will ruin this elegant soup. Use an immersion blender for best control, or blend in small batches in a regular blender.
  • Cream Quality: Use real heavy cream or crème fraîche. Avoid evaporated milk or cream substitutes—the richness is essential to the dish's character.
  • White Pepper: White pepper is more delicate than black pepper and won't show dark specks in the pale soup. This is traditional and matters for presentation.
  • Temperature Matters: Serve in chilled bowls. Cold soup tastes different than hot soup—it often needs slightly more seasoning. Always taste before serving.
  • Make Ahead: Vichyssoise actually improves with a day in the refrigerator as flavors meld together. Make it the day before for best results.
  • Serve Very Cold: This soup loses its appeal if served at room temperature or even barely chilled. It should be served straight from the refrigerator or served over ice.
  • Garnish Choices: Fresh chives are traditional, but you can also use crispy bacon bits, a drizzle of truffle oil, or crispy leek chips for variations.
  • Potato Quality: Use starchy potatoes like russets, not waxy potatoes. Starchy potatoes will break down and blend better for a silkier texture.
  • Vegetable Broth vs. Chicken: Vegetable broth is more traditional and lets the leek flavor shine, but chicken broth also works beautifully.
